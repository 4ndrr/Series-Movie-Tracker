1 Getting Started

Open the app

Visit the tracker's web address. On your very first visit you'll see a short welcome page — press Open the app. A quick interactive tour then points out the most important buttons; follow it or skip it, it only appears once.

Create your profile

Open the menu (☰) in the top-right, expand Profile and click Change User. Create a profile with a name and an optional picture URL. Each profile has its own list, watchlist and statistics.

Add your first title

Press the round red + button in the bottom-right corner and let auto-fill do the work — see the next section.

2 Adding Content

Manually, with smart auto-fill

Press +

Use the floating + button on the main page (or + Add Content in the menu) and choose Series or Movie.

Type two letters

Start typing the title — suggestions with cover art appear. Typing squi finds Squid Game. Click a suggestion or press Enter for the top match.

Everything fills itself

Cover, genre, seasons & episodes, runtime, rating, age rating, description and streaming services are filled in automatically. Pick a status, adjust anything you like, and press Save.

💡

Not in the suggestions? For series the app automatically asks the online TVMaze database as a fallback, so brand-new and obscure shows auto-fill too.

From the Marketplace

  • Open the menu → Marketplace to browse a catalogue of 1000+ popular titles.
  • Filter by category, provider, status or age rating — your filters are remembered for your next visit.
  • Click any title for full details: description, seasons, ratings, where to stream, and a Watch trailer link.
  • Press Add to library and choose: Currently Watching, Want to Watch or Finished.
  • Whole franchises can be added at once from the Collections row.

3 Tracking Progress

Log what you watch — the tracker keeps your exact place and builds your history.

Log an episode

  • Hover over a card you're watching — an action bar pops up and stays until you click it or hover another card.
  • Press Episode Watched: today's watch is logged and you advance to the next episode.
  • End of a season? The app asks whether to jump to the next one. End of the series? It's marked Completed automatically.

Edit anything

Press Edit in the action bar to change progress, rating (5 stars, half stars supported), notes, tags, dates, favourite actors or streaming services. Changes save automatically while editing. More opens the full detail panel.

Watch timer

Press and hold a card for about a second to start a watch timer. Set Max Time To Finish in the title's edit form first — when the timer ends, your progress is logged automatically.

4 Watchlist & Planning

Your queue

  • Add titles to the queue via Add to Queue in their edit form, or straight from the Marketplace.
  • The Watchlist section at the bottom of the main page shows your queue in priority order.
  • Drag any row up or down to reorder — on a phone, use the ☰ handle. Numbers show the order.

“What tonight?”

  • Press What tonight? at the top of the Watchlist section.
  • Optionally filter by genre and how much time you have (“a quick one” up to “an epic”).
  • Press Surprise me — the app picks for you. Not feeling it? Spin again, or press Start watching to commit.

5 Search, Filters & View Sizes

  • Search bar — searches titles and genres across your library as you type.
  • Status buttons — All, Currently Watching, Want to Watch, Completed. Want them colour-coded? Enable Colored Filters in menu → Settings.
  • Filters & Sort — sort by title or time spent, and filter by content type (series or movies).
  • View Size — Small, Normal, Large or Custom. The Custom slider zooms the entire card, so small sizes fit far more titles on screen.
  • Bulk operations — tick the checkboxes on multiple cards to move or delete them all at once.

6 Statistics & Year in Review

  • Stat cards — totals for your items, statuses, watch time and completion rate.
  • Genre breakdown — which genres dominate your library.
  • Watch Timeline — a graph of your logged sessions (lifetime, year or month). Hover a point to inspect it, click to pin.
  • Year in Review — the button under the timeline opens a “Wrapped”-style recap of any year: episodes and movies watched, total watch time, busiest months, top genres, most-watched titles and your longest daily streak.
📈

Statistics grow from logging — press Episode Watched as you go and your stats become more interesting every week.

7 Themes & Personalisation

  • Themes — menu → Themes: Dark, Light, or Custom.
  • Custom colors — set the background, accent and the three status-badge colors. Use the preset swatches, type a hex code, or open the full color wheel.
  • Gradient text — give all headings a color gradient with your own start and end colors.
  • Colored Filters — in menu → Themes; tints the status filter buttons in their theme colors.

8 Notifications & Calendar

  • Open menu → Notification Calendar and press + Add Notification to schedule a reminder for a new season or episode of any tracked title.
  • Edit or remove reminders in the same calendar; past entries hide automatically.
  • When a reminder's date arrives, the title moves to Want to Watch and updates to the newest season/episode.
  • Not interested in popups? Toggle Notifications off in Settings.

9 Backup & Data

Export & import

Export List in the menu downloads your library as a file. On another device or browser, use Import List and choose whether to import the list only, or also the profile name and picture.

Auto-Export

Switch Auto Export on in Settings — an interval picker appears (only while enabled) and your data is backed up automatically while the site is open.

⚠️

Your data lives in this browser. Clearing cookies/site data erases it — keep a recent export somewhere safe.

10 Install as an App

The tracker is a Progressive Web App: install it once and it opens full-screen from your home screen or dock, and keeps working offline.

  • iPhone/iPad (Safari): Share → Add to Home Screen.
  • Android (Chrome): menu (⋮) → Add to Home screen / Install app.
  • Desktop (Chrome/Edge): the install icon in the address bar → Install.
